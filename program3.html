Self-Education and Learning Program (SELP)

1. Introduction

Bangladesh is a developing country. Here, a vast number of people are not aware of their duties, responsibilities and rights and therefore they cannot experience individual independence and better life as they should have. The question is: How and where from they become concerned about it. According to Bangladesh Bureau of Statistics (BBS), there are about 41 million households/family are living in this country (HIES 2022) and most of these families do not have the ability to provide education and necessary socialization opportunity to their children. Thus, the only place where the children in Bangladesh actively learn norms, values, and behaviors in the schools, which shapes their attitudes and prepare them for Bangladesh. Unfortunate, the irresponsibility, corruptions, lack proper educational management, poor course curriculum and many untrained teachers in the schools/college/university can hardly provide good education to the children to build up them properly for the country. Though people are spending much money for their childrenâ€™s education, it does not function properly due to the poor education system of Bangladesh. However, the people are not aware of this fact that the children are not educated properly for their future by the system. They are not raising their voice against the pitfalls in the education system. Mainly the lack of accountability of the governing authorities has made this scenario in the country. Anyway, the citizens of the country need to raise their voice against the poor education system to reform it. On the other hand, the economy of a country grows faster when the social systems are strong enough due to required knowledge and skills of the people. People living in a country must have the mindset and ability to cope up with the right process of development. Thus, a government should take initiatives to develop proper human resources for the necessary development projects. When honesty and other moral values of the people in the society i. e. , the public integrity and personality is strong enough, their actions, thinking, opinions become standard to play the proper role for sustainable development of the country. However, it is significantly necessary to construct appropriate public integrity and personality through proper education system in Bangladesh. Therefore, to shape the mindset of our people Community Education and Research Network, Bangladesh (CERN BD) conducts the Self- Education Program (SELP). (Supported by Physics & Science Education (PSE) Lab, Physics department, JU, Savar, Dhaka 1342)

2. Objectives

  1. To motivate people for self-education and learning about socioeconomic systems.
  2. To enhance public integrity and human qualities in the community.

This program works among all level of citizens to construct high public integrity and personality to reduce corruption in the community for social security. The SELP also extends its work to build awareness among the citizens about their responsibilities and duties, and rights in the social systems and relevant affairs.

3. Activities

  • Focus group discussion and seminars are arranged by the SELP to interact and exchange ideas among students, professionals and elders.
  • Necessary surveys are carried out among the people over time to observe various social phenomena and trends.
  • Voluntary research assistants (RA) are employed to interact with interested individuals and groups in the community for research work.

4. Funding Process

Donation managed by internal process: This donation is deposited directly to a bank account number to arrange SELP for community people. The SELP team takes initiatives to arrange SELP at convenient venues. An internal audit is performed in this process to ensure the proper use of the funds for the program. Anyone can see the report through proper procedure.

1) Personal donations.
2) Agency/ Business group/ Organizational donations.
3) Crowdfunding.


Donation managed by external process: In this process the required expenditure for SELP and relevant cost is arranged externally by any individual/ group. SELP team provides necessary budget of the expenditure and intellectual support for the program. SELP team do not handle any cash for expenditure for the program/ seminar/ conference/ research. In this process donation is not deposited in the bank account.

5. Impact

  • People might become aware of own responsibility to reduce corruption and social problems.
  • It increases the peoplesâ€™ understanding about different social systems.
  • It helps individuals to produce strong desire, discipline and dedication for their own vision and mission development.
  • It helps the people to improve their integrity and personality.

6. Frequently Asked Questionnaire

1. What is the goal of the SELP?
Answer: The goal of the SELP is to engage the people for self-education and learning process that foster to produce the public integrity and personality.

2. What kinds of hands-on activities are included in the SELP?
Answer: The activities include to conduct public lectures or seminars/focus group or panel discussions regarding various topics which helps to understand social systems to promote the community interests.

3. What is the age group in the community for which the SELP works?
Answer: It works for all age groups of people from school students to the elders in the community.

4. What are the challenges that SELP faces?
Answer: Potential challenges include limited resources and lack of trained facilitators and to address the challenges, the SELP team always welcome the partnerships of the local organizations for funding, materials and volunteers to conduct the programs.

5. How does the SELP align with educational standards of the national curriculum?
Answer: The SELP organizes focus group discussions about community problems and for their solutions.

6. How do I donate for the SELP?
Answer: One can donate directly to bank account or any individual/ group can arrange the required expense for the SELP according to capacity.
