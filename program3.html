Self-Education and Learning Program (SELP)

1. Introduction

Bangladesh is a developing country. Many people here are unaware of their duties, responsibilities, and rights, which hinders their individual growth and national contribution. Though education is supposed to empower them, the existing system is plagued by poor curriculum, management, and corruption. SELP (Self-Education and Learning Program) by CERN BD aims to raise awareness and rebuild public integrity through Socratic dialogue, seminars, and voluntary learning.

2. Objectives

  • To motivate for Self-Education and Learning.
  • To provide political, social and economic understanding for civil betterment.
  • To develop Vision & Mission using 3Ds (Desire, Discipline, Dedication).
  • To build strong Public Integrity and Personality (PIP).

3. Activities

  • Seminars and focus group discussions with students, professionals, and elders.
  • Surveys on key issues in communities.
  • Involving TAs and RAs for research and teaching activities in communities.

4. Funding Process

Internal Donation Management:

  • Bank deposits managed by SELP team and internal audit.
  • Donation sources: individuals, businesses, crowdfunding.

External Donation Management:

  • Supporters manage full cost; SELP provides budget and guidance.
  • No money is handled by SELP in this process.

5. Impact

  • Encourages self-education among students and professionals.
  • Improves understanding of key national systems.
  • Inspires individuals to pursue Vision-Mission with discipline.
  • Builds personal and public integrity in communities.

6. FAQs

Q: What is the goal of the SELP?
A: To promote public integrity and personality through self-education.

Q: What activities are included?
A: Seminars, group discussions, and public lectures on community issues.

Q: Who can join?
A: Anyone — from school students to seniors.

Q: What challenges does SELP face?
A: Limited resources and facilitators; seeks local partnerships to overcome them.

Q: Does it align with national curriculum?
A: Yes, through discussions addressing community-based problems and awareness.

Q: How can I donate?
A: Either directly to the bank or by covering specific program expenses.
