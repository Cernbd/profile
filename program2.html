Science Education Program (SEP): Engaging Students Through Hands-On Science Experiments

There are thousands of schools and madrasas in Bangladesh where a huge number of students are studying in the science group, but only a few are government laboratory schools. Secondary education is mainly run by non-government schools and madrasas. In many schools the class size is too large for effective teaching. Most schools lack well-trained and skilled science teachers, science experiment labs, and proper governance systems—especially in rural areas. Poor funding, low teacher salaries, recruitment of unqualified teachers, and inefficient governing bodies have worsened the situation. In addition, the education system of Bangladesh has repeatedly undergone experimentation by different governments without considering contextual feasibility and people’s needs. The system still lacks modernization, development, and strong ethical education and practice. These factors have made the educational system too weak to produce honest, committed, and patriotic scholars. Therefore, a contextual and proper education system is needed to build qualified human resources. Traditional classroom teaching provides information passively, and students simply memorize textbooks without gaining the ability to apply their knowledge to real-life problems. To build curiosity, engagement, and understanding, hands-on practice of science experiments is essential. For this reason, our team—supported by the Physics and Science Education (PSE) Laboratory of the Department of Physics, Jahangirnagar University—runs the Science Education Program (SEP) to empower students to explore and engage in science through creativity and analytical ability.

Objectives:

The SEP conducts hands-on science experiment programs (based on school textbooks) for school students to help them explore and understand core scientific principles. The objectives of SEP are:


  1. To provide theoretical knowledge through interactive lectures.
  2. To actively demonstrate experiments by well-trained university students.
  3. To teach school students how to write project or experiment reports.
  4. To assess the performance of the students.

Methodology:

1) A selected number of brilliant university students from physics, chemistry, mathematics, and biology are trained through the Teachers’ Training Course (TTC). This training includes teaching maxims, inquiry methods, and research techniques such as qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods, along with statistical analysis. The SEP team must first demonstrate the experiments at CERN Center before presenting them in schools. 2) Lesson plans or manuals are properly prepared using inquiry-based learning methods. 3) Required theoretical knowledge is provided to students with sufficient explanation before each experiment so they understand the scientific principles. 4) School students conduct the designed experiments themselves to gain hands-on experience in observing scientific principles. 5) Assessment is conducted through interviews or questionnaires for each group of students participating in an experiment.

The Management Policy of the SEP:

1) PSE Lab and/or a school may contact one another to run SEP by signing an agreement. 2) The school must provide available laboratory resources and classroom or lab space. 3) A school science teacher (external evaluator) must assess the SEP activity and the SEP team’s performance using a prescribed form for research data—without any condition or payment. 4) A member of the SEP team must also assess the program and the performance of SEP team members. 5) The SEP team must arrange required experiment resources if the school lacks them, but space must be provided by the school. 6) No party may harm or damage each other’s equipment or property. SEP does not pay schools for the program. 7) Either party may cancel the program due to unavoidable circumstances, with prior notice. 8) SEP will not conduct programs in schools where there are security concerns. 9) The typical SEP duration is approximately two (2) hours. SEP Conducts the Experiments for SSC Examination:

Some examples are below.

  1. Measuring the dimensions of an object using Vernier Scale and Slide Calipers and calculating its volume.
  2. Measuring the critical angle of refraction.
  3. Identifying acids and bases using litmus paper.
  4. Observing the exothermic reaction of calcium hydroxide.

Note: Student assessment and primary data collection are performed by the SEP team for research under the Physics and Science Education Laboratory, Department of Physics, Jahangirnagar University.

Funding Process

Anyone can donate to the SEP directly or arrange the required tools during the program. An internal audit ensures proper use of funds. SEP collects funding through:

  1. Personal donations
  2. Agency/Business group/Organizational donations
  3. Crowdfunding

The Impact of SEP

  1. University students receive training and field experience for future teaching.
  2. School and college students explore science principles through hands-on activities.
  3. It increases creativity, analytical ability, and understanding of science among students.

Frequently Asked Questionnaire

  1. What is the goal of the SEP?
    Answer: The goal is to engage school students in practical, interactive science learning that fosters curiosity, critical thinking, and scientific literacy.
  2. How does hands-on learning benefit students?
    Answer: Hands-on experiments allow students to explore science directly and connect it to real-world applications. This improves understanding, knowledge retention, problem-solving skills, and teamwork.
  3. What age group does SEP work with?
    Answer: SEP currently works with secondary school students.
  4. What challenges does SEP face and how are they addressed?
    Answer: SEP faces challenges such as limited resources and lack of trained facilitators. Partnerships with local organizations help provide funding, materials, and volunteers.
  5. How do I donate to SEP?
    Answer: Anyone or any group may donate directly to SEP or provide educational tools and equipment for school programs. The SEP team provides intellectual support according to their capacity.
