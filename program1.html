Community Education Program (CEP)

For School and College Students

Bangladesh is a small and very densely populated country. This large population makes challenges to maintain different systems of the country properly. An efficient education system is very necessary to build up qualified human resources for a country. However, the education system of Bangladesh is being experimented many times without considering the contextual feasibility and the needs of the people. This has made the educational system too weak to produce honest, committed and patriotic scholars who will serve the country properly. The current classroom teaching methods provide information to the students in the schools passively. Many schools, especially the non-government schools, cannot maintain proper educational standards and environment without having required funding and expert teachers. The students memorize from the textbooks and learn something for good grades, but they are not able to use their knowledge to solve real life problems. Their behavior, creativity and analytical thinking are not being shaped for the betterment of the community. Hence, improving students’ understanding through proper teaching methodology is very important. The inquiry method is one of the most relevant teaching methods applicable in schools. For this purpose, the Community Education Program (CEP) is conducted to improve the students’ critical and analytical thinking such that they can apply their knowledge properly in real life. This program is supported by the Physics and Science Education (PSE) laboratory, Department of Physics, Jahangirnagar University.

Objectives:

The CEP provides ‘after school teaching’ around the week at the CERN center using inquiry method in the community mainly for the school and college students. The followings are the objectives of the CEP: 1. To provide proper concepts and understanding to the students based on the syllabus of the National Curriculum and Textbook Board (NCTB) of Bangladesh. 2. To demonstrate scientific experiments by well-trained university students.

Working Process:

  1. University students from disciplines such as Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Biology, Bangla, and English are trained for teaching assistantship under the TTC (Teachers’ Training Course) program. This coursework includes teaching-learning maxims, research methods, and statistical data analysis.
  2. Qualified TTC students are recruited as Teaching Assistants (TAs). They teach using standard lesson plans and tools applying the inquiry method.

    [The inquiry method is a student-centered approach encouraging learners to explore, investigate, and ask questions. Students actively engage by conducting experiments and using critical and analytical thinking instead of receiving answers passively.]
  3. School and college students are enrolled to participate in daily CEP schooling for three hours (4:00 pm to 7:00 pm).
  4. Schooling is conducted routinely based on the NCTB syllabus.
  5. Students perform designed experiments to explore scientific laws.

Assessment Policy in the CEP:

  1. A weekly MCQ exam and biweekly written exams with creative questions are conducted.
  2. A well-organized team of Research Assistants (RAs) monitors the teaching and assessment system carefully.
  3. Research data is collected from examinations, experiments, and guardian interactions.
  4. RAs analyze the data to improve teaching strategies and CEP policies.

Funding Process:

Donation can be deposited directly to a bank account number to support poor students under CEP. An internal audit is done to ensure proper use of the funds. Reports are available through proper procedure. The CEP receives funds through: 1) Personal donations 2) Agency / Business group / Organizational donations 3) Crowdfunding

The Impact of CEP:

  1. University students get training for future teaching and contribute to the education system.
  2. It increases students’ critical and analytical thinking for solving problems.
  3. Poor families in the community benefit by educating their children.

Frequently Asked Questionnaire:

1. What is the goal of the CEP?

Answer: To engage school students to make them creative and problem solvers for the community.

2. Which students can get admission under CEP?

Answer: Currently, CEP works mainly for secondary school students, but it will be extended for primary to college (HSC) students in future.

3. How do you deal with poor students in the CEP?

Answer: The CEP waives fees for poor students based on their economic condition.

4. How do I donate for the CEP?

Answer: Donations can be made through educational tools, experimental equipment, logistic support, or paying on behalf of poor students. Volunteering for teaching or research is also welcome.
